Learn the basic rules of Cipher!

The object of the game is simple: Destroy your opponent's lord.

The game is set out as a war between two players, as they deploy units to destroy the other's army. Each "army" is a deck of 50 cards, with no more than 4 copies of the same card.

Each player starts out by placing one 1 cost unit from their deck onto the battlefield in the Vanguard Area. This unit is that player's lord, and is usually marked with another card below it. Each player then shuffles their deck and draws 6 cards. They can then choose to "mulligan", or shuffle their hand back into the deck and redraw the same amount of cards. Player's then decide by their method of choosing which player will go first (ie. R-P-S or coin flip). Once the turn order has been decided, players set aside 5 orbs in the orb area. These orbs represent the amount of times each player's lord can be attacked before defeat, which will be explained in detail later.

Once setup has been completed play can commence. Each player's turn consists of 5 phases;
  1. Beginning Phase
  2. Bond Phase
  3. Deployment Phase
  4. Action Phase
  5. End Phase

Here is a reference to what the set up should look like:

Beginning Phase

In the Beginning phase, players do the following:
  • Activate any skills which text say "at the start of your turn"
  • Untap all units
  • Draw one card

Bond Phase

The Bond phase is where players can choose one card in their hand and place it face up in the bond area. Players may only play one bond per turn.

Deployment Phase

In the Deployment phase, players can tap any number of bonds to deploy a unit from their hand. A player may also class change a unit they have on the field by tapping a number of bonds equal to the class change cost. A unit cannot be deployed if there is already a unit with the same name as that unit on that player's side of the field. At least one bond tapped must be the same color as the unit being deployed or class changed. Flipped bond cards have no color associated.

The Deployment cost is listed in the colored, larger bubble and the class change cost is listed below in a smaller orb. When a unit is class changed, the player taps the required bonds, stacks the new card on top of the unit being class changed, and draws one card. The class changed unit must have the same name as the original unit (ie Byleth(f) cannot be class changed into Byleth(M)).

The deployment cost of this Byleth(F) is 5, while the class change cost is 4.

Action Phase

The action phase covers many options for units. Each unit can activate their skills any number of times (limitted by their card text) as long as the player pays the cost for each skill. Skills can be activated at any point during the action phase.

During the action phase, each untapped unit can tap to do one of the following:

  • Move into another area on that player's side of the battle
  • Attack an enemy unit in range

Combat
When an ally attacks an enemy unit, each player flips one card from the top of their deck and places it in the support area. The Support value of the card (located on the bottom right) is added to the attack value of that player's unit's attack (located in the bottom left) in combat. The support skill of the supporting card may activate depending on the conditions of the skill (ie, attacking or defending). If the attacking unit's attack value is higher or equal to the defending's, the defending unit is destoyed and sent to the retreat area. If the defending unit's attack is higher, the fight ends without either unit being destroyed. If the defending unit was the player's lord and the unit is destroyed, that player takes one orb from their orb pile. If the defending player's lord is defeated and has no orbs left, the attacking player wins the game.

The attack value of this Seliph is 40, and his support value is 20. Seliph also has a support skill that activates when your unit is attacking!

Critical/Evasion
When attacking/defending, player's may discard a card from their hand with the same name as the unit attacking/defending to preform a critical hit or an evasion. If your unit is attacking and you discard a same name card from your hand, your unit preforms a critical hit and their attack value (plus support value) is doubled. If your unit is defending and you discard a same name card from your hand, your unit "evades" the attack and is not destroyed at the end of the combat, regardless of the attack values.

End Phase

During the end phase, player's may activate any skills that specify activation during this phase, and play passes to the next player.
